# Visual Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before final delivery and when revising generated covers.

## Mobile Feed Readability

- Can the main title be understood in 1-3 seconds?
- Is the main title still readable when shrunk to phone feed size?
- Are there fewer than 3 competing focal points?

## Business Fit

- Does the cover make the target audience obvious?
- Does it show a clear click reason: pain, result, product, proof, person, or comparison?
- Does it support the desired action: save, follow, DM, trial, buy, comment, or share?

## Photo Quality

- Is the face clear and not covered?
- Does the expression match the title emotion?
- Is the body proportion natural?
- Are hands and fingers acceptable if visible?

## Material Use

- Is each uploaded material assigned a clear role?
- Is the product/prototype readable enough to act as proof?
- Are unnecessary screenshots, logos, or cards removed?

## Brand Continuity

- If past covers are provided, are title color, outline, layout logic, and account tone consistent?
- If no past covers are provided, does the visual direction fit the business profile?

## Common Failure Fixes

- Title too long: compress to 4-12 Chinese characters.
- Product screenshot too cluttered: crop one core area or turn it into a background proof.
- Person too weak: enlarge the face, use stronger expression, or add identity label.
- Too ad-like: replace promotional words with concrete pain/result language.
- Too generic: add audience word, number, result, or contrast.
