# Cover Types

Use these directions as options. Recommend, but let the user choose when the strategic priority is unclear.

## 高点击冲击版

Best for traffic, hot takes, tutorials, and painful problems.

- Large title takes visual priority.
- Strong facial expression and high contrast.
- Few but loud proof elements.
- Risk: can look cheap or too aggressive for premium consulting.

## 专业可信版

Best for consultants, B2B services, founder IP, high-ticket offers, and serious business topics.

- Person looks calm and competent.
- Title is still large but cleaner.
- Materials show proof: framework, case, dashboard, product screen, or client outcome.
- Risk: can become too bland if the hook is weak.

## 产品展示版

Best for products, prototypes, tools, apps, templates, and AI workflows.

- Product screenshot/prototype is the proof object.
- Person points to or frames the product.
- Use 1-3 callout tags, not too many tiny interface details.
- Risk: screenshot can become unreadable or steal focus from the title.

## 人设强化版

Best for personal brand, repeated account format, consultants, educators, and founders.

- Person is the primary visual anchor.
- Identity label or short credential can appear near the person.
- Past cover colors and layout rules should be reused when provided.
- Risk: if the title is generic, the cover becomes self-centered instead of audience-centered.

## 测评对比版

Best for product review, tool comparison, rankings, before/after, and selection guides.

- Use numbers, ranking cards, comparison tags, or score labels.
- Product materials can surround the person or form a grid.
- Title should contain a number or a clear comparison frame.
- Risk: too many product logos or dimensions reduce readability.

## 课程交付版

Best for course, training, consulting package, templates, and paid deliverables.

- Show course outline, template preview, result screenshot, or deliverable mockup.
- Emphasize outcome rather than "course".
- Risk: can look like hard advertising; use concrete result language.

## 案例结果版

Best for client stories, revenue result, growth diagnosis, and transformation posts.

- Use a before/after contrast, metric, timeline, or case card.
- Person should look analytical or confident.
- Risk: compliance and credibility; avoid fake exaggerated claims.
